Movie Review

01.

KPOP DEMON HUNTERS

Kpop Demon Hunters is honestly such a wild and fun ride. Imagine your favorite K-pop idols moonlighting as badass demon slayers. It’s so stylish, action-packed, and doesn’t take itself too seriously which makes it super entertaining. The animation is slick, the colors pop, and the fight scenes are choreographed and the dance performances were so good. The characters are lovable and you get that perfect mix of humor, friendship, and just enough emotional moments to ground the story. It’s definitely not just for K-pop fans either. If people who likes supernatural action with a twist of pop culture flair, this is the one.

02.

BARBIE

Barbie (2023) is way more than just pink heels and dreamhouses. Imagine stepping into a glittery plastic world only to be hit with deep questions about identity, purpose, and womanhood. Yep, that’s the vibe. The visuals are stunning like every scene could be a magazine spread but it’s the script that really shines. It’s hilarious and self-aware, poking fun at Barbie’s legacy while celebrating her impact. Margot Robbie is perfect in the role and Ryan Gosling as Ken totally steals the show. What's excited in this film is the balances fun and feminism, comedy and commentary. It’s not just for kids or nostalgia lovers, it’s for anyone who's ever questioned where they fit in. Barbie is way more than what people expect and that’s what makes it brilliant.

03.

THE MONKEY

The Monkey (2025) is creepy, gore, and way more unsettling than you'd expect from a toy monkey with cymbals.This films is very cinematic vibes. Based on a Stephen King short story, the movie takes a simple nostalgic object and turns it into a full blown nightmare. The atmosphere is heavy with dread from the start, and the pacing keeps you hooked. Every time those cymbals clash, you know something bad’s coming and that tension just builds. I love the way how the characters slowly unravel as the monkey’s curse creeps in. It’s not overly gory or loud, but it plays with psychological horror really well. This film is about their childhood trauma with a supernatural twist. If people who are into horror films that messes with your mind more than your stomach, The Monkey is definitely worth the watch.

04.

KARATE KID LEGENDS

Karate Kid: Legends (2025) honestly surprised me in the best way. It brings back that classic Karate Kid vibe, but makes it feel current without trying too hard. Seeing some of the OG characters team up with the new generation? Super satisfying. The chemistry between them just works. The fight scenes feel real not just cool choreography, but like they actually mean something. There's a lot of emotion packed in, especially when it comes to legacy and figuring out who you are. It’s not just about karate, it’s about growth, family, and respect. Whether you grew up with the originals or are just jumping in now, this one hits that sweet spot. It's got heart, it’s got action, and yeah by the end, you’ll probably wanna bow and do a crane kick too.

05.

THUNDERBOLTS

Honestly, Thunderbolts feels like Marvel finally decided to get a little gritty and it works. It’s not your typical shiny superhero movie. It’s the messy, a bit chaotic, and way more character focused than I expected. Yelena (Florence Pugh) steals every damn scene. She brings this sharp mix of humor and pain that makes you care about her instantly. And Sentry? Lowkey creepy in the best way that definitely adds tension every time he’s on screen. The team feels like a bunch of misfits forced to work together, and watching that dynamic unfold is half the fun. It’s less about saving the world and more about messed-up people trying not to fall apart. Not perfect, but way more interesting than most recent Marvel stuff.
